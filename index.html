Hello, I’m Maxine.

I build software, teach when it matters, and care deeply about systems that last.

The short story

I’m a backend-leaning software engineer with a strong bias for clarity, structure, and delivery. I like work that sits at the intersection of technology, people, and long-term impact—especially education, platforms, and systems that need to grow without breaking.

I’ve learned that good engineering is rarely loud. It’s calm, deliberate, and often invisible once it’s done right.

What I’ve spent my time doing

Over the years, my work has taken a few different shapes:

  • Building and shipping production features as a freelance engineer, often working end-to-end and owning outcomes.
  • Teaching programming at Algorithmics Hub, where translating complex ideas into simple, intuitive explanations became a core strength.
  • Working within a high-traffic, real-world environment at Betika, where reliability, teamwork, and execution under pressure matter.

Each of these sharpened a different muscle—but they all reinforced the same thing: systems are only as good as the people who can understand and maintain them.

How I tend to work

  • I ask why before how, and I document what I learn.
  • I’m comfortable in ambiguity, but I don’t stay there.
  • I prefer simple architectures that scale over clever ones that don’t.
  • I care about users, even when I never meet them.

Quiet signals

I’ve been trusted to work without hand-holding, to teach others without ego, and to make technical decisions that outlive my involvement.

I think in trade-offs. I write code with the next engineer in mind. I don’t panic when things break—I debug, fix, and improve the system.

Titles matter less to me than ownership, responsibility, and impact.

Outside the obvious

  • I enjoy teaching and mentoring because it forces clarity.
  • I’m drawn to mission-driven work, not just feature factories.
  • I believe good software should feel boring in production.
